## Conflict of Interest and Financial Conflict of Interest (FCOI) Policy
Effective Date: January 01, 2026
Approved By: Thomas Kelly, Director

Applies To: All Investigators and Key Personnel participating in externally sponsored research
N.B.: Please contact the director for the most up-to date version of this document.

---
## 1. Purpose
Icy Seas Co-Laboratory (“the Organization”) is committed to promoting objectivity in research and ensuring that the design, conduct, and reporting of research are free from bias resulting from Investigator financial conflicts of interest.
This policy establishes requirements for disclosure, review, management, and reporting of actual or potential conflicts of interest in accordance with:
- 42 CFR Part 50, Subpart F (“Responsibility of Applicants for Promoting Objectivity in Research”) 
- 45 CFR Part 94 (“Responsible Prospective Contractors”)

This policy is active and enforced for all sponsored research activities conducted by or through the Organization.

---

## 2. Definitions
### 2.1 Investigator
An Investigator is the Project Director/Principal Investigator and any other person, regardless of title or position, who is responsible for the design, conduct, or reporting of research funded by a sponsor, including but not limited to employees, contractors, and collaborators working under an agreement issued to the Organization.

### 2.2 Significant Financial Interest (SFI)
A Significant Financial Interest means a financial interest consisting of one or more of the following interests of the Investigator (and the Investigator’s spouse and dependent children) that reasonably appears to be related to the Investigator’s institutional responsibilities:

1. Remuneration received from a publicly traded entity in the 12 months preceding disclosure and the value of any equity interest in that entity, when aggregated, exceeds $5,000.
2. Remuneration received from a non-publicly traded entity in the 12 months preceding disclosure that exceeds $5,000, or any equity interest in such entity.
3. Intellectual property rights and interests (e.g., patents, copyrights) upon receipt of income related to such rights and interests.
4. Sponsored or reimbursed travel related to institutional responsibilities, except where excluded by regulation (see Section 2.4).

### 2.3 Financial Conflict of Interest (FCOI)
A Financial Conflict of Interest (FCOI) exists when the Organization, through its designated review process, reasonably determines that an Investigator’s SFI could directly and significantly affect the design, conduct, or reporting of sponsored research.

### 2.4 Exclusions (Not SFI)
The following are not considered SFIs under this policy:

- Salary, royalties, or other remuneration paid by the Organization
- Income from seminars, lectures, or teaching engagements sponsored by a U.S. federal, state, or local government agency, a U.S. institution of higher education, an academic teaching hospital, a medical center, or a research institute affiliated with a U.S. institution of higher education
- Income from service on advisory committees or review panels for the same types of entities listed above
- Travel reimbursed or sponsored by the same types of entities listed above

---
## 3. Policy Requirements
### 3.1 Disclosure Requirement
Each Investigator must disclose all SFIs that reasonably appear to be related to their institutional responsibilities.

Disclosures must be made:

1. No later than the time of proposal submission for sponsored research.
2. At least annually during the period of award.
3. Within 30 days of discovering or acquiring a new SFI (e.g., through purchase, marriage, inheritance, or new paid relationship).
### 3.2 Disclosure Format
Disclosures must be made using the Organization’s Conflict of Interest Disclosure Form, which requires sufficient detail to determine whether an SFI exists and whether it is related to the sponsored research.

---
## 4. Review and Determination Process
### 4.1 Designated Official

The Organization designates a Conflict of Interest Officer (COI Officer) to implement and enforce this policy. The COI Officer is responsible for:

- Receiving Investigator disclosures
- Determining whether an SFI exists
- Determining whether the SFI is related to the sponsored research
- Determining whether an FCOI exists
- Developing and monitoring management plans
- Coordinating required reporting to prime recipients and sponsors

The COI Officer may consult external experts as needed.

### 4.2 Conflict of Interest Committee (Optional)
When appropriate, the COI Officer may convene an ad hoc Conflict of Interest Review Committee (e.g., Executive Director plus two independent advisors) to support review and management decisions.

### 4.3 Determinations

The COI Officer will determine:
1. Whether the disclosed interest meets the definition of an SFI
2. Whether the SFI is related to the sponsored research
3. Whether the SFI constitutes an FCOI
Determinations will be documented and retained in accordance with Section 11.

## 5. Management of Financial Conflicts of Interest
### 5.1 Management Requirement
All identified FCOIs must be satisfactorily managed, reduced, or eliminated prior to the expenditure of any funds under a resulting agreement, and in a timely manner sufficient to enable required FCOI reporting.
### 5.2 Management Plan
When an FCOI is identified, the Organization will develop a written FCOI Management Plan. Management plans will be tailored to the nature of the conflict and may include one or more of the following:

- Public disclosure of the financial interest (e.g., in publications and presentations)
- Disclosure of the conflict to project personnel
- Appointment of an independent monitor capable of reviewing the research objectively
- Modification of the research plan
- Change of personnel responsibilities
- Disqualification from participation in the portion of the research affected by the conflict
- Reduction or elimination of the financial interest (e.g., divestiture)
- Severance of relationships that create the conflict

### 5.3 Monitoring
The Organization will monitor Investigator compliance with the management plan on an ongoing basis until the completion of the sponsored project.

---
## 6. Subrecipient and Prime Recipient Coordination

When Icy Seas Co-Laboratory is a subrecipient under a prime award, the Organization will comply with the prime recipient’s FCOI reporting timelines and will provide FCOI information necessary for the prime to meet its obligations to the sponsor.

The Organization will, upon request, provide:

- Certification that an active and enforced COI policy is in place
- Confirmation that all required disclosures have been obtained
- Confirmation that identified FCOIs have been managed prior to expenditure of funds
- Documentation of management plans (as allowable under privacy and administrative constraints)

---
## 7. Reporting of FCOIs
### 7.1 Reporting to Prime Institution

If an FCOI is identified for a project funded through a prime recipient, the Organization will provide the prime recipient with sufficient information to support timely reporting as required by the sponsor.
### 7.2 Timing

The Organization will provide FCOI reporting information:

- Prior to the expenditure of funds on the project whenever an FCOI exists
- Within 60 days of identifying a new FCOI during the project period
- As otherwise required by the sponsor or prime recipient

---
## 8. Training Requirements

Each Investigator must complete FCOI training:

- Prior to engaging in sponsored research under this policy, and
- At least every four years, and immediately when:
    - The Organization revises this policy in a manner that affects Investigator requirements
    - An Investigator is new to the Organization
    - An Investigator is found noncompliant with this policy or an FCOI management plan

Training may be satisfied by completion of a recognized online module (e.g., NIH FCOI tutorial) or Organization-provided training.

---
## 9. Noncompliance and Remedies
### 9.1 Failure to Disclose

Failure to disclose an SFI as required is considered noncompliance and may result in:

- Suspension of participation in the sponsored project
- Required retrospective review of research activities
- Corrective actions required by the sponsor or prime institution
- Termination of the Investigator’s role in the project

### 9.2 Retrospective Review and Mitigation
If the Organization determines that an FCOI was not identified or managed in a timely manner, the Organization will complete a retrospective review consistent with federal requirements and will implement a mitigation plan as required.

---
## 10. Confidentiality
Financial disclosures will be treated as confidential and will be shared only with individuals who require access for the purposes of review, management, monitoring, and reporting, or as required by law or sponsor requirements.

---
## 11. Record Retention
The Organization will maintain records of all Investigator disclosures, review determinations, and management plans for at least:

- Three (3) years from the date of submission of the final expenditures report, or
- Such longer period as required by the prime award terms, sponsor requirements, or applicable law
