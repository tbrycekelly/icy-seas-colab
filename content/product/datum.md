# A durable system of record for research data

Research data rarely arrives as one clean, final package. Files are uploaded in waves, corrected, renamed, reorganized, documented, and prepared for multiple destinations. Datum is designed to preserve that history while helping a research team move toward a trustworthy release.

## What Datum does

- Organizes projects, data records, files, and file revisions
- Collects uploaded files into documented, versioned datasets
- Compares dataset versions and identifies additions, removals, modifications, and renames
- Validates metadata against reusable schemas and reports release readiness
- Publishes immutable releases with review checklists
- Produces portable ZIP exports with manifests, metadata, and checksums
- Provides project and record permissions, API tokens, and audit history
- Supports browser, API, and command-line workflows

## Why it matters

The final archive deposit is only one moment in the life of a dataset. Datum is being built for the work before that moment: reconciling changing files, making documentation visible, assigning responsibility, and retaining enough provenance to explain how a release was assembled.

## Current boundaries

Datum is under active development and is not yet offered as a general self-service product. Direct multipart uploads, rich file previews, advanced spreadsheet reconciliation, and broader authentication and deployment options remain on the roadmap.
